Our charter

The political and strategic foundations of BAF Camp

Why this camp

BAF Camp is a space to meet, organise and strengthen struggles. It stems from an observation: rights are being eroded, repression is on the rise and movements are fragmented.

The aim is to forge links between collectives, share practices and build strong, lasting alliances, whilst maintaining vibrant and accessible spaces.

What we aim to do

🤝

Connect

Build bridges between struggles and regions (Brussels, Wallonia, Flanders).

📢

Raise awareness

Highlight local and international struggles.

🧠

Organise

Share strategies, debate and build common ground.

Positioning

The camp is part of social, anti-fascist, ecological, peasant, feminist, anti-racist, anti-imperialist, decolonial, anti-ableist and queer struggles. It is based on an anti-authoritarian approach and seeks to limit power relations, including internally.

  • Rejection of oppression (racism, sexism, ableism, classism, LGBTQIA+phobias…)
  • Mindful of power dynamics in discussions
  • Central role given to those directly affected
  • No dominant promotion of any single organisation or ideology

Collective operation

The camp is self-managed and relies on active participation. It operates through working groups and decisions taken collectively.

  • Horizontal organisation (consensus-based approach)
  • Participation by all (volunteer shifts during the camp)
  • Open to new people and groups

Basic framework

The camp is not a guaranteed ‘safe’ space, but a space where we try to create conditions of respect and listening.

  • Respect for personal experiences and voices
  • Consent (interactions, photos, physical contact)
  • Rejection of violent or manipulative behaviour
  • Taking responsibility in the event of a conflict

A team dedicated to care and conflict management will be present throughout the camp.

Accessibility & inclusion

Efforts are being made to make the camp accessible to as many people as possible, although not everything will be perfect.

  • Translations FR / NL / EN
  • Attention to specific needs (diet, accessibility, etc.)
  • Spaces with a chosen mix of participants
  • Working with relevant groups in advance
