Practical

Everything you need to know

Getting there, sleeping and eating—everything you need to know to come and stay at BAF Camp 2026.

🚧 Site under construction — full and final details will be announced soon!

📍 How to get there

Getting to the festival

🚆

Train

The site is a 300-metre walk from Grupont station

🚲

Cycle

You can cycle to the venue. Parking and repair equipment available on site.

🚗

Car

Direct access to the site by car is available at the following address: Chem. de Lavaux 1, 6927 Tellin, Belgium. Free on-site parking; carpooling encouraged.

🍴 Food & drink

Catering

Canteen arrangements have not yet been finalised. If you’re part of a collective that can help with equipment and expertise,please get in touch!

⛺ Accommodation

Camping

The campsite is open from 17 to 26 August, on a grassy field just a stone’s throw from the camp venue.

A camping area exclusively for MINT (Women, Intersex, Non-binary and Trans) people will be set up.

🗓️
Opening dates

Arrival possible from Sunday 16 August from 2pm. Departure by Tuesday 26 August at 12 noon at the latest.

🧒 Little activists

La Bambinerie

BAF Camp is for families too! A dedicated area for children aged 4 to 16 will be available on site, so parents can enjoy the talks and workshops with peace of mind.

🌅
Morning slot

A two-hour morning slot is available for dropping children off at La Bambinerie whilst you attend the programme’s talks and workshops.

🌤️
Afternoon slot

A second two-hour slot in the afternoon is available to allow parents to make the most of the day.

🎨
Activities for little ones

Outside of the childcare slots, certain organised activities can be adapted for toddlers: games, crafts, discovery activities… BAF Camp is for everyone!

💬
Any questions about the Bambinerie?

Please don’t hesitate to contact us at activismcampbelgium@proton.me with any questions regarding childcare.
